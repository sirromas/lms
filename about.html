What is Globalization Plus?

Globalization Plus is a nonpartisan online current events teaching tool that contains assignments that are especially designed for use by students. Published weekly throughout the September — June school year, the digital newsmagazine is designed as a course supplement to be used during class or as a homework assignment.

The convenient weekly schedule ensures that teachers can remain focused on their core curriculum while also providing students with current events reports that are frequent enough to ensure that they are aware of the most important developments in the news.

What are the assignments?

Every week, Globalization Plus highlights a new current events topic. Each edition features an assignment that has been designed as a self-contained online teaching module so that they can be easily completed by students without the need for any extra instructor support. Whether used simply as an in-class exercise, the basis for classroom discussion, or strictly as homework, instructors can choose the approach that is most effective for them and yet in all cases, offering Globalization Plus to their students guarantees that they will acquire an impressive grasp of current events.

Each edition of Globalization Plus highlights one major news topic. Every multimedia assignment contains 4 parts that combine text, video, student engagement, and testing consisting of the following:

  • 1. an online News Backgrounder article providing students with crucial facts about the selected topic
  • 2. an online Video News Report presenting a dramatic firsthand look at unfolding developments
  • 3. an online Discussion Board that nurtures critical thinking by allow students to share their opinions
  • 4. a downloadable News Quiz for in-class completion that encourages a careful examination of the featured story
  • plus every edition also features The Globalization Plus Political Dictionary as a current events reference resource
How is it graded?

Students are automatically credited for their Discussion Board postings and graded on the results of the News Quiz. Teachers simply go to their personalized Gradebook to view or download the student grades.

Simple to assign and easy to assess, Globalization Plus delivers convenient supplemental current events instruction especially created for busy instructors with demanding core curricula and crowded schedules.

Who chooses the content?

The curriculum coordinator for Globalization Plus is Professor Steve Posner, Ed.M., Harvard University (click here for his bio). He teaches advanced writing for International Relations and Global Economics at the University of Southern California and created Globalization Plus for use by his own students. Professor Posner is the recipient of two distance learning research grants from USC's Center for Scholarly Technology, and was the founding editor of the Marshall School of Business online student journal titled ﻿Insight Business. In addition to his on-campus work at USC, he also teaches a special summer distance learning course especially designed for student interns working at various locations around the world. Professor is the author of five books, including a highly praised exploration of the Middle East conflict published by Syracuse University Press.

Is it objective?

Globalization Plus is published by Executive Clarity, a widely respected educational publishing company that has produced nonpartisan current events materials for use by millions of students in classrooms across the United States. With more than three decades of experience, it staff has created classroom materials that have been praised by both liberals and conservatives, Democrats and Republicans, independents and observers across the ideological spectrum, including President George Bush, President Bill Clinton, former Democratic Secretary of Education Richard Riley, and Republication Education Secretary Education Lamar Alexander. Dedicated to serving students in both public and private schools by offering teachers practical current events teaching tools that have no political or religious bias of any kind, the company's publications have even been distributed overseas by the Peace Corps and the United States Information Agency for use in classrooms throughout South Asia and Eastern Europe, and in China in partnership with the Los Angeles Times.
