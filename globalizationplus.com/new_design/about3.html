What is NewsFacts & Analysis?

NewsFacts & Analysis is a nonpartisan online current events teaching tool created by Professor Steve Posner to address the current events gap among college students identified in his post-presidential election research project which surveyed colleagues throughout the United States. Professor Posner's research revealed a significant lack of news awareness among college students which surprisingly includes political science majors attending the nation's most prestigious universities.

Published weekly throughout the September—May academic year, NewsFacts & Analysis is especially designed to serve as an inexpensive course supplement, carefully crafted to provide reliable news for students without burdening professors with time-consuming lesson plans, preparation, and grading.

Professor Posner teaches Advanced Writing for International Relations & Global Economics at the University of Southern California. "The focus of my course is on writing and not current events instruction," he explains, "yet even a slight increase in news literacy among students offers us an opportunity to underscore the relevance of political theory and history to today's headlines."

The convenient weekly schedule of topical nonpartisan content and brief assignments allows an instructor to remain focused on his or her core curricula while also providing students with current events reports that help keep them updated on the most important developments in the news.

What are the assignments?

Each issue features an assignment that has been designed as a brief, simple-to-use online teaching module so it can be easily completed by students without the need for any extra instructor support. Whether used simply as an in-class exercise, the basis for classroom discussion, or strictly as homework, instructors can choose the approach that is most effective for them and yet in all cases, offering NewsFacts & Analysis to their students guarantees that they will acquire an impressive grasp of current events.

Each edition of NewsFacts & Analysis highlights one major news topic. Every multimedia assignment contains four parts that combine text, video, student engagement, and testing consisting of the following:

✓News Backgrounder providing students with crucial facts about the selected topic

✓Video News Report presenting a dramatic firsthand look at unfolding developments

✓Discussion Board that nurtures critical thinking by allow students to share their opinions

✓News Quiz for in-class completion that encourages a careful examination of the featured story

✓The NewsFacts & Analysis Political Dictionary provides a current events reference resource

How is it graded?

Students are automatically credited for their Discussion Board postings and graded on the results of the News Quiz. Teachers simply go to their personalized Gradebook to view or download the student grades.

Simple to assign and easy to assess, NewsFacts & Analysis delivers convenient supplemental current events instruction especially created for busy instructors with demanding core curricula and crowded schedules.

Is it objective?

NewsFacts & Analysis is published by Executive Clarity, a widely respected educational publishing company that has produced nonpartisan current events materials for use by millions of students in classrooms across the United States. With more than three decades of experience, its staff has created classroom materials that have been praised by both liberals and conservatives, Democrats and Republicans, independents across the ideological spectrum, including President George H.W. Bush, President Bill Clinton, former Democratic Secretary of Education Richard Riley, and former Republication Education Secretary Lamar Alexander who now serves as Senator from Tennessee.

Dedicated to serving students in both public and private schools by offering teachers practical current events teaching tools that have no political or religious bias of any kind, the company's publications have even been distributed overseas by the Peace Corps and the United States Information Agency for use in classrooms throughout South Asia and Eastern Europe, and in China in partnership with the Los Angeles Times.
